I'll highlight where the distinction between a 'brief' and a 'PRD' typically lies.** --- # Project PRD / Project Brief: [Project Name] * **Document Type:** [Select One: Product Requirements Document (PRD) / Project Brief] * **Version:** 1.0 * **Date:** October 26, 2023 * **Author(s):** [Your Name/Team] * **Stakeholders:** [List Key Teams/Individuals: e.g., Engineering Lead, Design Lead, Marketing, Sales, Customer Support, Executive Sponsor] --- ## 1. Executive Summary * **Brief:** A concise, high-level overview of the project, its purpose, and expected impact. Focus on the "what" and "why." * **PRD:** A slightly more detailed summary, introducing the problem, the proposed solution, and the key outcomes. --- ## 2. Problem Statement * **Brief:** Clearly articulate the core problem you are solving. Focus on the user's pain point and the business impact. * **PRD:** Expand on the problem with supporting data, user research, or competitive analysis where available. Define *who* has the problem and *what* the consequences are. --- ## 3. Vision & Goals/Objectives * **Vision:** What is the ideal future state or long-term aspiration this project contributes to? (e.g., "To make financial planning effortless for young professionals.") * **Goals/Objectives:** * **Brief:** List 2-3 SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) business goals. * **PRD:** Detail the SMART goals. Include how these goals tie back to company strategy and potentially link to specific KPIs. --- ## 4. Target Audience / Users * **Brief:** Describe the primary user segments or personas who will benefit from/use this product/feature. * **PRD:** Provide more detailed user personas, including their needs, behaviors, pain points, and current workflows related to the problem. --- ## 5. Scope (In-Scope & Out-of-Scope) * **Brief:** Clearly list the major components or features that *will* be part of this initial release (in-scope) and what *will not* be included (out-of-scope), helping manage expectations. * **PRD:** Provide a detailed breakdown of in-scope features, specifying what functionality is required. Explicitly list out-of-scope items to prevent scope creep. --- ## 6. Features & Functionality (Core Requirements) * **Brief:** List the core features at a high level. (e.g., "User Login," "Product Search," "Payment Processing"). * **PRD:** This is the most detailed section. For each feature, include: * **User Stories:** (As a [type of user], I want to [action] so that [benefit]). * **Acceptance Criteria:** Specific conditions that must be met for the story to be considered complete. * **Functional Requirements:** Detailed descriptions of what the system must do. * **Non-Functional Requirements:** (Performance, Security, Scalability, Usability, Accessibility, Localization). * **UI/UX References:** Link to wireframes, mockups, design specifications. --- ## 7. User Experience (UX) & User Interface (UI) Considerations * **Brief:** Mention the desired user experience (e.g., "intuitive," "seamless," "modern"). * **PRD:** Detail specific UX principles, usability requirements, accessibility standards (WCAG), and refer to design artifacts (wireframes, prototypes, design system components). --- ## 8. Technical Considerations (Primarily for PRD) * **Brief:** (Often omitted, or a very high-level note like "must integrate with existing CRM.") * **PRD:** Outline technical requirements, architectural considerations, integration points with other systems (APIs), data models, security requirements, and any specific technology choices or constraints. --- ## 9. Success Metrics (Key Performance Indicators - KPIs) * **Brief:** List the top 2-3 measurable metrics that will indicate the success of the project. * **PRD:** Detail the primary and secondary KPIs. Specify how they will be tracked and measured, baseline metrics, and target values. --- ## 10. Phased Rollout / Milestones / Timeline * **Brief:** Provide a high-level timeline or key milestones for the project. * **PRD:** Link to a detailed project plan if available. Otherwise, outline phases, key dependencies, and target dates for major deliverables. --- ## 11. Dependencies & Risks * **Brief:** Note any critical external dependencies (e.g., "Requires third-party API access") and major foreseeable risks. * **PRD:** Detail all internal and external dependencies (teams, systems, data, partners). List potential risks (technical, market, operational), their likelihood, impact, and proposed mitigation strategies. --- ## 12. Open Questions / Assumptions * **Brief:** List any major assumptions being made or key questions that still need to be answered. * **PRD:** Detail all assumptions (technical, market, user behavior) and any outstanding questions that need resolution before or during development. --- ## 13. Appendices (Primarily for PRD) * **Brief:** (Typically not included) * **PRD:** Links to supporting documentation such as: * User Research / Data Analysis * Competitive Analysis * Market Research * Wireframes / Mockups / Prototypes * Technical Design Documents * Analytics Dashboards * Glossary of Terms --- **Please provide your context, and I'll generate the document for you!**
